---
title: "Root Causes"
description: "The personal, social, political, and economic factors that researchers associate with increased risk of radicalization."
---

No single factor explains terrorism, but several pressures can increase risk. Researchers often examine personal, social, political, and economic conditions together. Radicalization may develop gradually, especially when multiple pressures and influences combine over time.

## Six contributing factors

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Ideology" icon="compass">
    Strong belief systems can shape how a person understands conflict, identity, and the use of violence.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Religion" icon="book">
    Extremist groups may misuse religious language to justify violence and recruit followers.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Politics" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    Political anger, instability, or extreme movements can create conditions that support radical views.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Financial Struggles" icon="shield">
    Poverty, unemployment, and limited opportunity can increase stress, frustration, and social isolation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Power and Control" icon="network-wired">
    Some groups use fear to gain influence, attract attention, or control others.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Social Environment" icon="users">
    Family stress, social exclusion, online influence, and community conflict can shape dangerous pathways over time.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
