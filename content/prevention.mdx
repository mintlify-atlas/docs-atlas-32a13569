Prevention works best when education, trust, and shared responsibility come together.

<Tip>
  Communities can lower risk by building trust, strengthening communication, and offering support systems before harmful ideas turn into harmful actions.
</Tip>

## Prevention ideas

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Promote Media Literacy" icon="book">
    Teach students how to question false information, online propaganda, and manipulative messages.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Strengthen Community Support" icon="users">
    Safe schools, mentoring, and counseling can reduce isolation and build trust.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Encourage Reporting" icon="shield">
    People should know how to report serious concerns through trusted school, campus, or public safety channels.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Build Inclusive Spaces" icon="network-wired">
    Respectful classrooms and community programs can reduce alienation and strengthen belonging.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Community action checklist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Use clear classroom discussion">
    Teachers and student leaders can use careful language, context, and fact-based examples.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Support students early">
    Counseling, mentoring, and peer support can help when stress, isolation, or grievance begins to grow.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Partner with families and local groups">
    Trust grows when schools, families, and community organizations share information and support.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Know where to report urgent concerns">
    Serious threats should be reported through campus safety, school leadership, or public safety systems.
  </Step>
</Steps>
