title: "Radicalization"
description: "How radicalization develops step by step and where early intervention can make a difference."
---

There is no single formula for radicalization, but many cases show a gradual process. That is why early support, trusted relationships, and accurate information are so important.

<Warning>
  Radicalization often develops over time through repeated exposure, pressure, and isolation rather than in a single moment.
</Warning>

## The four-stage path

<Steps>
  <Step title="Grievance or frustration">
    A person may feel angry, isolated, treated unfairly, or disconnected from others.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Repeated harmful messaging">
    Extremist media, online propaganda, or group pressure can slowly reinforce extreme ideas.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Growing isolation">
    When trusted support becomes weaker, extreme identity or us-versus-them thinking may become stronger.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Early intervention matters">
    Education, mentoring, counseling, and community support can interrupt the path before harm occurs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

</Tip>
