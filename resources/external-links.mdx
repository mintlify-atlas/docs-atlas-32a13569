## What this resource supports

The featured resource from the Global Investigative Journalism Network covers three core areas of academic need:

- **Background reading** — Build foundational knowledge before class discussion or written work.
- **Research support** — Find additional sources to strengthen your arguments and analysis.
- **Presentation preparation** — Identify examples, data points, and framing strategies for your class presentations.

## How to use these resources effectively

### For class research

Start by identifying the specific subtopic you are researching — such as root causes, radicalization patterns, or prevention strategies. Use the external resource guide to locate articles and reports that speak directly to that subtopic. Take notes on author credentials, publication date, and the organization behind the source.

### For class discussion

Before participating in discussion, read at least one outside source to bring a factual reference point into the conversation. Use neutral language when summarizing what you read, and distinguish between what the source reports and what you personally interpret.

### For presentations

Draw on the external resource guide to find credible examples that illustrate the concepts from the main website. Cite your sources clearly, and avoid sensational or emotionally charged language when presenting findings to your audience.

<Tip>
  Investigative journalism organizations like GIJN apply professional standards for accuracy and sourcing. Check whether any article you use has been independently verified or cross-referenced with primary data.
</Tip>
