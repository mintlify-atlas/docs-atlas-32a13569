**Understanding Terrorism: Root Causes and Prevention** is a college Criminal Justice and Marketing project built as an educational website for students, educators, and campus audiences. It explains terrorism in neutral academic language, compares domestic and foreign terrorism, traces the root causes researchers examine, and highlights awareness and prevention as the primary community response.

<Info>
  All content uses factual, neutral language designed to support classroom discussion and campus-level research — not to sensationalize or stereotype.
</Info>

## What this project is

This site was created for a college-level course in 2026 by four students: **Jean Segura**, **Brandon Serna**, **Danilo Nikolic**, and **Tomas Vega**. It presents the topic in a serious, respectful, and readable format that works well for research, discussion, and classroom presentation.

The site covers terrorism in clear academic terms — from definition and types, through root causes and radicalization, to awareness and prevention strategies grounded in education and community support.

## Who it's for

- **Students** researching terrorism for Criminal Justice, Political Science, or Sociology courses
- **Educators** looking for a structured, neutral classroom resource
- **Campus audiences** who want factual context and prevention guidance

## Academic approach

This project follows three core principles drawn directly from its source content:

<Note>
  A careful definition matters because it helps students focus on behavior, intent, and public safety rather than stereotypes about religion, nationality, race, or identity.
</Note>

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Neutral Explanation** | The website uses factual language to support academic learning and respectful classroom discussion. |
| **Respect for Communities** | The content avoids stereotypes and keeps the focus on harmful behavior, not identity. |
| **Prevention First** | Each section returns to awareness, education, and support as the strongest community response. |
