This page documents the purpose, academic context, and guiding principles of the **Understanding Terrorism: Root Causes and Prevention** project — a college presentation website built with a prevention-centered message.

## Purpose and academic context

This project was created for a college Criminal Justice and Marketing course in 2026. It presents terrorism as an academic subject using clear, neutral language — structured for students, educators, and campus audiences who need factual context, not sensationalism.

<Note>
  This is a college showcase project. All content is written for classroom discussion, research support, and presentation preparation. It does not represent the views of any institution or government body.
</Note>

The site is designed to work well for research, discussion, and classroom presentation. It uses simple academic English, clear sections, and a respectful tone throughout.

## Why studying terrorism academically matters

<Info>
  A careful definition matters because it helps students focus on behavior, intent, and public safety rather than stereotypes about religion, nationality, race, or identity.
</Info>

Academic study of terrorism gives students the tools to distinguish between reliable information and propaganda, understand complex social phenomena without resorting to stereotypes, and recognize how prevention — not fear — is the most effective community response.

## Study principles

These three principles guide the content, tone, and structure of every page on this site.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Neutral explanation">
    The website uses factual language to support academic learning and respectful classroom discussion. Every section avoids loaded terms or framing that could distort the subject.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Respect for communities">
    The content avoids stereotypes and keeps the focus on harmful behavior, not identity. Religion, nationality, race, and personal identity are never treated as causal factors.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prevention first">
    Each section returns to awareness, education, and support as the strongest community response. Prevention is framed as a shared responsibility — not a reaction to fear.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  When using this site for classroom discussion, pair each content section with the prevention and awareness pages to reinforce the project's core message: understanding leads to prevention.
</Tip>
